Predicting Stock Price using RNNs
    • Summary
    • This project is to predict the stock price of AMAZON using RNN method. The goal is to use stock price data (open, high, low, and close data) from the prior 60 days to predict the close price of the stock in 5 days. By evaluating the results of different method, we have have a brief view of the performance of each models.

RNN Model 1

Recurrent neural network with one SimpleRNN layer, which includes 128 units in the SimpleRNN layer.

Compile Adam optimizer( learning rate = 0.0001).

To know the performance of the model, I use mean absolute percentage error or the mape metric on the train and validation sets. The number of the training epoch is 50.

    The Mean Absolute Error metric (MAE) is 0.0765. Recall that MAE is simply equal to mean(abs(predictions - targets)). Since our data has been centered to 1 by devided it's maximum, we have a mae of 0.0765*134.18 =$ 10. 26477

RNN Model 2

Recurrent neural network with one LSTM layer, includes 128 units in the LSTM layer.

Compile Adam optimizer( learning rate = 0.0001).

To know the performance of the model, I use mean absolute percentage error or the mape metric on the train and validation sets. The number of the training epoch is 50.

The Mean Absolute Error metric (MAE) is 0.0166. Recall that MAE is simply equal to mean(abs(predictions - targets)). Since our data has been centered to 1 by devided it's maximum, we have a mae of 0.0166*134.18 =$ 2.2273

RNN Model 3

Recurrent neural network with one LSTM layer, with recurrent dropout.

Compile Adam optimizer( learning rate = 0.0001).

To know the performance of the model, I use mean absolute percentage error or the mape metric on the train and validation sets. The number of the training epoch is 50.

The Mean Absolute Error metric (MAE) is 0.0166. Recall that MAE is simply equal to mean(abs(predictions - targets)). Since our data has been centered to 1 by devided it's maximum, we have a mae of 0.0394*134.18 =$ 5.28669
